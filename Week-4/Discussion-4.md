## Instructions

Sampling bias can significantly impact the validity of research findings in higher education. For this discussion, you will analyze real-world examples of sampling issues and their consequences.

---

## Initial Post Requirements (Due: February 9, 2026)

In your initial post (minimum 300 words), address the following:

1. **Research Example:** Find and describe a published higher education study where sampling methodology could affect the generalizability of results. This could be from academic journals, institutional research reports, or national surveys.

2. **Sampling Method Analysis:**
   - What sampling method was used?
   - Who was included/excluded from the sample?
   - What potential biases might exist?

3. **Impact Assessment:** How might sampling limitations affect:
   - The conclusions drawn?
   - Policy decisions based on the research?
   - Future research in this area?

4. **Improvement Recommendations:** Suggest specific ways the sampling approach could be improved while remaining practical and ethical.

---

## Response Requirements (Due: February 11, 2026)

Respond substantively to at least two classmates' posts:
- Do you agree with their assessment of potential biases?
- Can you identify additional sampling concerns they may have missed?
- How might the sampling issues they identified affect different stakeholder groups?
