# Midterm Assessment

**Due:** February 11, 2026

---

## Overview

The midterm assessment evaluates your understanding of statistical concepts covered in Weeks 1-4. You may choose ONE of the following options:

---

## Option A: Literature Review (120 points)

### Purpose
Demonstrate your ability to critically analyze statistical methods used in higher education research.

### Requirements

1. **Article Selection:** Choose 5-7 peer-reviewed articles from higher education journals that employ quantitative methods

2. **Analysis Components:**
   - Identify research questions and hypotheses
   - Describe statistical methods employed
   - Evaluate data presentation and interpretation
   - Assess strengths and limitations of each approach

3. **Synthesis:** Compare and contrast methods across articles

4. **Reflection:** Discuss implications for your own research interests

### Format
- Length: 8-10 pages (double-spaced, excluding references)
- APA 7th Edition format
- Include reference list and in-text citations

### Grading Criteria

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Quality and relevance of article selection | 20 points |
| Depth of statistical method analysis | 30 points |
| Critical evaluation of strengths/limitations | 30 points |
| Synthesis across articles | 20 points |
| Writing quality and APA formatting | 20 points |

---

## Option B: Comprehensive Exam (120 points)

### Purpose
Demonstrate mastery of statistical concepts through a timed examination.

### Exam Format
- **Duration:** 2 hours
- **Format:** Mixed (multiple choice, short answer, calculation problems)
- **Open-book:** You may use your textbooks and notes

### Topics Covered
- Descriptive statistics and data visualization
- Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode)
- Measures of variability (range, variance, standard deviation)
- Probability concepts and rules
- Normal distribution and z-scores
- Random variables (discrete and continuous)
- Sampling methods and bias

### Grading Criteria

| Section | Points |
|---------|--------|
| Multiple Choice (20 questions) | 40 points |
| Short Answer (5 questions) | 40 points |
| Calculation Problems (4 problems) | 40 points |
