# Final Project: Comprehensive Statistical Analysis

**Due:** March 11, 2026
**Points:** 150

---

## Overview

The final project is your capstone assignment demonstrating comprehensive understanding of statistical concepts and their application to higher education research. You will conduct an original analysis addressing a research question of significance to the field.

---

## Project Requirements

### Part 1: Introduction and Research Question (20 points)

- Identify a higher education issue or question of interest
- Provide context with relevant literature (3-5 sources minimum)
- State clear research questions or hypotheses
- Explain the significance of this research for practice or policy

### Part 2: Data and Methodology (25 points)

- Describe your data source and variables
- Explain your analytical approach and justify statistical methods chosen
- Discuss any data cleaning or preparation steps
- Address limitations of your data

### Part 3: Descriptive Analysis (25 points)

- Present appropriate descriptive statistics for all key variables
- Include relevant visualizations (tables, charts, graphs)
- Interpret what the descriptive statistics reveal about your data
- Discuss any notable patterns or distributions

### Part 4: Inferential Analysis (40 points)

Conduct at least TWO of the following types of analyses:
- Correlation analysis
- Simple or multiple regression
- Independent or paired samples t-test
- One-way ANOVA

For each analysis:
- State hypotheses
- Check assumptions
- Report full statistical results (test statistics, p-values, effect sizes)
- Interpret findings in context

### Part 5: Discussion and Implications (25 points)

- Summarize key findings
- Connect results to your research questions
- Discuss implications for higher education practice or policy
- Acknowledge limitations of your study
- Suggest directions for future research

### Part 6: Professional Presentation (15 points)

- APA 7th Edition formatting throughout
- Clear, professional writing
- Proper citation of all sources
- Logical organization and flow
- Appropriate use of tables and figures

---

## Submission Requirements

- **Format:** Word document or PDF
- **Length:** 12-18 pages (excluding references and appendices)
