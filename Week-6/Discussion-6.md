## Instructions

Understanding the proper interpretation of statistical significance is crucial for evidence-based decision making in higher education. This discussion explores how p-values and significance are often misinterpreted.

---

## Initial Post Requirements (Due: February 23, 2026)

In your initial post (minimum 300 words), address the following:

1. **Common Misinterpretations:** Describe at least two common misinterpretations of p-values or statistical significance. Explain why these interpretations are incorrect.

2. **Real-World Example:** Find an example from higher education research or institutional reporting where:
   - Statistical significance was properly interpreted, OR
   - Statistical significance was misinterpreted or overemphasized

   Explain your assessment.

3. **Statistical vs. Practical Significance:** Provide an example where a result could be statistically significant but not practically significant (or vice versa) in a higher education context.

4. **Recommendations:** What practices would you recommend for properly communicating statistical findings to non-statistical audiences (such as administrators, faculty, or students)?
